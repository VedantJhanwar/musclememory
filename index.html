The Story

Memory is a liar. It reshapes the past to fit the person we’ve become. But what happens when the body remembers something the mind has wiped clean? When every scar tells a story that doesn't match the life you recall living.

In this gripping psychological thriller, the line between identity and delusion blurs until it vanishes completely. Truth isn't found in what you remember—it's hidden in what you can't forget.

A Note from the Author

Writing this book was an exploration of the terrifying fragility of self. We assume we are the sum of our memories, but biological reality suggests otherwise. I wanted to explore that disconnect—the horror of discovering a stranger within your own skin.

— Vedant J.
